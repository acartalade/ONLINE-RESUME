Skills and areas of modeling

Mathematical and numerical methods

Phase-field modeling and Lattice Boltzmann Methods

Phase-field theory: Variational calculus for minimization of energy functional with Lagrange multiplier \(\bullet\) Derivation of interface-capturing models with their constitutive laws (see [L1]) \(\bullet\) Various applications such as two-phase flows (see e.g. Navier-Stokes/conservative Allen-Cahn model for immiscible two-phase flow) with or without phase change, crystal growth, dissolution of porous media and surfactant, Ostwald ripening \(\bullet\) Asymptotic expansions between sharp interface and diffuse interface [P12] \(\bullet\) Implementation in LBM_Saclay, verifications with Stefan’s problem & benchmarks.

Lattice Boltzmann Methods: derivation and implementation of numerical schemes for various PDEs (Navier-Stokes, Advection-diffusion, fractional Equations [O8], phase-field models, …): modifications of Equilibrium distribution function \(\bullet\) Chapman-Enskog expansion [P7] \(\bullet\) Practice of BGK, TRT and MRT [P10] collision operators \(\bullet\) Modification of the streaming stage with the Lax-Wendroff scheme \(\bullet\) Adaptative Mesh Refinement with LBM \(\bullet\) Programmer of a 3D LBM Fortran code SILABE3D [H1] (2009–2018) \(\bullet\) Involved in the development of the C++ multi-architecture HPC code LBM_Saclay



The PDEs, derived from the phase-field theory and combined with LBM, are implemented in the LBM_Saclay code


Other mathematical methods

Direct problems: implementation and practice of finite difference method [R1] & [R2] and finite element methods [T1] with a direct solver \(\bullet\) Euler explicit & implicit time-schemes \(\bullet\) Implementation of random walks and Continuous Time Random Walks. Volume Averaging Methods.

Inverse problems: adjoint state method and optimization for identification of PDEs parameters \(\bullet\) derivation of adjoint problems in continuous and discrete forms \(\bullet\) Fortran implementation in a 2D finite element code [T1] and a finite difference code [R1] & [R2] \(\bullet\) Coupling with optimization algorithms e.g. with Hessian approximation such as BFGS with bound constraints \(\bullet\) Application for fractional equations [P5] & [P9].

High Performance Computing & Informatics

Programming skills: developer of SILABE3D (Fortran 77/90/2000) and LBM_Saclay (C++), makefile, CMake \(\bullet\) Version control system with git, debugger \(\bullet\) architecture portability with Kokkos \(\bullet\) Coupling with math librairies (lapack, slatec, numerical recipes).

Post-processing: paraview, gnuplot, python.

Writing documentation with Sphinx and reStructuredText (.rst) files \(\bullet\) web deployment with GitHub pages.

High Performance Computing (HPC): practice of MPI commands in SILABE3D. Involved in the development of LBM_saclay: parallelization with MPI and multi-architecture portability with Kokkos library [R18]. Batch scripts (SGE, slurm);

Operating System: Linux, shell scripts, Windows. Analytical calculus: wxmaxima

Writing skills: LaTeX, BibTeX, LyX \(\bullet\) presentations with Beamer, tikz; figures with Matcha.

Areas of modeling and simulations

Computational Fluid Dynamics and thermodynamics

Single phase flows: Navier-Stokes (NS) \(\bullet\) Incompressible flows and low Mach number \(\bullet\) Eq. of state for compressible flows formulations \(\bullet\) Natural convection with Boussinesq approximation [R8] \(\bullet\) Non-Newtonian flows [O5].

Immiscible two-phase flows: phase-field models for 3D bubbles dynamics & buoyancy \(\bullet\) Spinodal decomposition, Phase separation, Nucleation and growth, with NS/Cahn-Hilliard model \(\bullet\) NS/Conservative Allen-Cahn model for 3D Rayleigh-Taylor instability, rising and 3D falling droplet, 2D Taylor bubble, splash.

Two-phase flows with phase change: phase-field model for liquid/gas phase change [P11], Film Boiling. Two-phase with surfactant: surfactant effect on coalescence, rising and falling droplets. Two-phase interacting with a solid phase: Leak of static and moving tank and droplet on wetting and non-wetting surface

Three-phase flows: Three-phase spinodal decomposition \(\bullet\) Spreading lens \(\bullet\) 2D simultaneous splashing droplet with Rayleigh-Taylor instability or rising droplet \(\bullet\) Simulations of surface tension effect for 3D separation of three immiscible fluids.

Materials science and thermodynamics

Solid-liquid phase transition: phase-field models simulated by LBM for 3D crystal growth [P7]; pure substance [R12] and binary mixture [R13] \(\bullet\) Functionals of free energy and grand-potential [P12] \(\bullet\) Anisotropic surface tension and Three interacting crystals [P8] \(\bullet\) «Enthalpy-porosity» approach for solidification. Coupling with fluid flow: simuls of phase-field model for 3D crystal growth with fluid flow effect [P6].

Liquid-liquid mass transfer: Ostwald ripening with fluid flow effect [P13] \(\bullet\) derivation of phase-field model for ternary phase diagram and simplification for modeling the thermodynamic database (Calphad).

Dissolution and precipitation: phase-field model of Dissolution of porous media (pore-scale) of binary [P12] and ternary mixtures.

See other videos of simulations in the LBM_Saclay’s documentation (Videos gallery of simulations with LBM and run_training_lbm)

Flow and transport in porous media

Flow and Transport in (un)saturated porous media: hydrogeology of CEA-Cadarache Fig. 2 – watershed scale) [T1] \(\bullet\) Dual-porosity models (Fig. 3 – column scale) [R7] \(\bullet\) Effective diffusion (Pore-scale) [R10] \(\bullet\) Darcy-Brinkman-Forchheimer model [R8] and equation of Richards \(\bullet\) Hydrodynamic dispersion.

Fractional Advection-Diffusion models: LBM for fractional Eq. (PDEs with derivatives of real order in space) [P5] and time [P9] Gaussian vs fractional \(\bullet\) Validations and comparisons with equivalent random walks. Inverse problem on fractional parameter.
